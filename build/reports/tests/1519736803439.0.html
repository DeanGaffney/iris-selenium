First Les Paul.

PhillipL, 05.09.2013
I was never a a fan of Les Pauls but I`d thought I`d try this model for a change. My other guitars are various Ibanez RG signature models plus a Dean RC7X and a Caparison Applehorn.
The Axcess heel is not really that much better than the usual heel joint but quite comfy all the same. The neck is quite a bit chunkier than all my other shred type planks but nice to play and the fretwork is very good for this end of the market. You know what you`re getting with the EMG pickups-serious chunk and cutting treble with the 81 in the bridge and smooth and creamy with loads of sustain with 85 in the neck. This thing has got almost as much sustain as Nigel Tufnel`s Les Paul Custom!
I`ve found that I`m using this as my main teaching guitar for standard tuning (and Drop D) as opposed to my Caparison which sells for six times the price of this guitar!
For this money (at least £60 cheaper in Thomann than in the UK) you can`t go wrong. This is a very good budget Metal type guitar which is actually used by Matt Heafy having seen Trivium a few times recently. Can`t recommend this guitar highly enough. Buy one yesterday-you know it makes sense!
p.s. If you buy one and send a photo of it to Matt Heafy`s Twitter feed he`ll show it on Twitter and follow you as well.

The BEST Les Paul Custom YOU will ever possess!

Nitronium Blood, 02.09.2017
Hey folks, I own two Epiphone Signature Les Paul Customs:

- Bjorn Gelotte Les Paul Custom which I purchased in December 2016
- Matt Heafy Les Paul Custom which arrived yesterday (1 September 2017) from Thomann

I can honestly say that this Les Paul is WITHOUT A DOUBT THE BEST guitar I have ever owned and played! It sounds and plays significantly better than the Bjorn Gelotte Custom (which is priced premiumly compared to the Matt Heafy Custom). I suppose the existence of a maple top does help with tone after all.

I wish it came with some sort of basic case (like the Bjorn Gelotte). Other than that... this guitar is ALL PROS, NO CONS.

If you are looking for a well built 'heirloom' quality instrument that does not bear a heirloom price tag, one that is a joy to play right out of the box then look no further.

I live in Perth, Australia and it took 12 days in total to reach me.

This is my very first purchase from Thomann and I am very impressed at the ease of it all.
